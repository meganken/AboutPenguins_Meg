All about Penguins

What are they

Penguins are a type pf flightless aquatic bird that typically live in the southern hemisphere. There are 18 different speices of penguin and most of they do not live in the true antarctic.

Basic Penguins

The basic penguin is what can be defined as common features among penguins. Antarctic penguins have the common white and black coats, other types of penguin can vary in colour such as blue or brown. All penguins share similar structures in order to be able to cut through water.

Primative Penguins

In the past, penguins were considered primative birds due to their flightless nature. TRhen it was discovered that they are highly evolved in order to live is extremely cold enviroments.

Food chain

Penguins are omnivors who eat fish and certain types of plants. They are not considered Apex predetors meaning that they are not at the top of the food chain and may be eaten by other animals

Habitats

Native to southern hemispheres, they are typically found in the antarctic. They can also be found in warmer areas such as New Zealand, Australia, South Africa and parts if south America.
